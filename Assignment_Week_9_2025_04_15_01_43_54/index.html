Manhattan Pocket Parks

Discover Hidden Green Spaces in the Concrete Jungle

Welcome to Manhattan's Secret Gardens

Manhattan has dozens of pocket parksnestled between skyscrapers and busy streets. These oases offer peaceful retreats from the city's hustle and more hustle. Enjoy a few moments of peacefulness!

Park 1 Image

Paley Park

53rd Street between Madison & Fifth

It has a 20-foot waterfall and honey locust trees.

Park 2 Image

Greenacre Park

East 51st Street between 2nd & 3rd

There is a dramatic 25-foot waterfall, lush plantings, and comfortable seating in a tranquil setting.

Park 3 Image

McGraw-Hill Pocket Park

West 48th Street & 6th Avenue

A hidden courtyard next to the McGraw-Hill Building with tables, chairs and plants.

Park 4 Image

Tudor City Greens

East 42nd Street & 1st Avenue

Two elevated parks with gardens, pathways, and benches overlooking Midtown.

Park 5 Image

50th Street Commons

West 50th Street near 8th Avenue

A serene space with benches and trees giving shade and quite from busy Midtown.

Park 6 Image

550 Madison Park

East 55th Street & Madison Avenue

A glass-covered public atrium with plantings, water features, and seating areas.

Park 7 Image

Septuagesimo Uno

West 71st Street between Broadway & West End

One of Manhattan's smallest parks with a few benches in this hidden sanctuary.

Park 8 Image

Amster Yard

East 49th Street between 2nd & 3rd

A historic courtyard garden that is part of the Spanish Institute.

Park 9 Image

Berlin Wall Remnant Park

520 Madison Avenue

Features an actual section of the Berlin Wall, preserved as a monument to freedom.

Park 10 Image

Labyrinth of Contemplation

Battery Park City

A peaceful walking labyrinth designed for meditation and reflection near the waterfront.
